How We Score Email Marketing Software in 2026

Every score in these email marketing software rating updates 2026 comes from the same six-category framework we have used since 2023 — but we updated the weighting this year to reflect how small businesses actually use these tools. Too many rating systems over-reward features that only large marketing teams touch. We do not do that.

Here are the six categories and their weights for 2026:

  • Ease of use (25%): Can someone with zero email marketing experience set up and send their first campaign within one hour? We timed real first-time users.
  • Template quality (15%): Do the built-in templates look professional on both desktop and mobile, without needing a designer to fix them?
  • Automation depth (20%): Can you set up basic welcome sequences, birthday emails, and re-engagement campaigns without hitting a paywall or needing a tutorial from a developer?
  • Deliverability rate (20%): What percentage of emails actually reach the inbox — not the spam folder? We use third-party inbox placement tests across Gmail, Outlook, and Apple Mail.
  • Customer support quality (10%): Can you reach a real human when something goes wrong? How fast, and through what channels?
  • Value for money (10%): Does the price reflect the features you get, especially at the entry tier that most small businesses actually use?

We test every platform with a live account, real contact lists, and actual campaigns. Scores are not based on vendor marketing materials or self-reported data.

What Changed in the 2026 Ratings Compared to Last Year

The biggest single shift driving the 2026 email marketing software rating updates was the enforcement of stricter sender authentication requirements by Google and Yahoo, which took effect in early 2025 and matured throughout the year. Platforms that made it easy for small businesses to set up DKIM, SPF, and DMARC records held their scores. Platforms that left users to figure this out alone dropped — sometimes by a full point in the deliverability category.

The second major change was the arrival of genuinely useful AI writing assistants inside email platforms. In 2024, most AI tools inside these platforms were gimmicks: they produced generic subject lines and bland body copy. By mid-2026, two platforms have AI assistants that small business owners consistently report as actually saving them time. We weighted this into our template quality and ease-of-use scores.

Pricing also shifted. Two platforms raised prices without adding meaningful new features. One platform dropped its price after losing market share. We updated all value-for-money scores to reflect current pricing as of June 2026.

Mailchimp: Still Strong, But No Longer the Best Value

Mailchimp drops to second place in our 2026 ratings, down from a tie with Constant Contact in our mid-2025 interim scores. The main reason for the drop: Mailchimp raised prices in February 2026 and eliminated its free tier for new accounts, while Constant Contact improved deliverability and added new templates at the same price point.

Mailchimp still scores well for templates. The Creative Assistant feature — which uses AI to pull brand colors and fonts from your website and apply them to templates automatically — is genuinely impressive and unique in the market. If you change your brand style frequently, Mailchimp makes that easier than any other tool we tested.

Where Mailchimp continues to struggle is customer support. At the entry tier ($13 per month as of June 2026), you get email support only, with typical response times of 24 to 48 hours. There is no live chat and no phone support unless you upgrade to a higher tier. For a small business owner who needs a quick answer on the day they are sending a campaign, this is a real limitation.

MailerLite and Brevo: The Budget Alternatives

MailerLite and Brevo both score in the 3.9–4.1 range in our 2026 ratings and earn their place in the comparison for one primary reason: price. MailerLite starts at $9 per month. Brevo starts at $9 per month as well, with a generous free tier that allows up to 300 emails per day.

MailerLite is the better of the two budget options. It has a cleaner interface, stronger template quality, and better deliverability than Brevo. The automation builder is capable for a platform at this price — you can build multi-step welcome sequences and simple conditional branches. The main limitation is customer support: MailerLite's support is email-only, and their knowledge base, while comprehensive, assumes a certain level of technical comfort that not every small business owner has.

To see whether the price saving is worth the trade-offs against Constant Contact, read our Constant Contact vs. MailerLite comparison, which includes a real cost-benefit calculation for a business with 500 contacts.

Brevo's main strength is its SMS marketing integration. If you want a single platform that handles both email and SMS campaigns, Brevo is the most affordable way to do that. However, its email deliverability scores have been inconsistent in our tests — we recorded inbox placement rates as low as 87% in some test batches, which is below the industry average. For businesses where every email counts, that inconsistency is a risk.

ActiveCampaign: Best for Power Users, Not for Beginners

ActiveCampaign earned the highest single-category score in our entire 2026 review — a 4.9 for automation depth. If you are running complex drip campaigns with behavioral triggers, lead scoring, and CRM integration, ActiveCampaign is the most powerful tool in this comparison and it is not particularly close.

But that power comes with a steep learning curve. In our first-use testing, new users took an average of 87 minutes to send their first campaign from signup — more than twice as long as Constant Contact. The interface is dense, the terminology assumes marketing experience, and the onboarding flow is designed for teams rather than solo business owners.

ActiveCampaign's entry price is also $15 per month — higher than Constant Contact — and the features that make it genuinely worth the price only become accessible on the $49-per-month Plus plan. At the entry tier, you are paying more for a harder-to-use product.

What Small Businesses Actually Need From Email Marketing Software

Before you finalize any decision based on these email marketing software rating updates 2026, it helps to be honest about what your business actually needs. Most small businesses do not need 500-step automation workflows or AI-powered predictive send-time optimization. Here is what most small businesses genuinely use email marketing for:

  • A monthly or weekly newsletter to keep existing customers engaged and coming back.
  • A welcome email sequence for new subscribers — typically two to four emails over two weeks.
  • Promotional emails for sales, seasonal events, or new product launches.
  • A re-engagement campaign to win back customers who have not bought in 90 days or more.

Every platform in our comparison can handle all four of those use cases. The question is which one lets you do them quickly, reliably, and with someone to call when something goes wrong before a big campaign. That is why Constant Contact wins for most small businesses — not because it has the most features, but because it makes the essential features the most accessible.

  • If you are just getting started with email marketing and you want to send professional campaigns without a steep learning curve: choose Constant Contact.
